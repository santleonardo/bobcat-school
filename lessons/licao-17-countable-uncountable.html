LIÇÃO 17 — Countable and Uncountable Nouns

Nível A1
🎯 Objetivo da Lição

• Diferenciar countable (contáveis) de uncountable (incontáveis) nouns.

• Usar a/an, many, How many com countables e much, How much com uncountables.

• Aprender measure words (a cup of, a glass of, a piece of, etc.) e quantifiers (a lot of, too much, too many).

🔥 Warm-Up: Comida contável ou não?

Pense em alimentos. Quais você pode contar? Quais não?

1. Apple — posso contar: 1 apple, 2 apples, 3 apples ✅

2. Rice — não posso contar: 1 rice ❌ → a bowl of rice ✅

3. Water — não posso contar: 1 water ❌ → a glass of water ✅

4. Egg — posso contar: 1 egg, 2 eggs ✅

5. Milk — não posso contar: 1 milk ❌ → a carton of milk ✅

💡 Dica: Se pode colocar número na frente (1, 2, 3...), é countable. Se não, é uncountable!

🗣️ Diálogo: At a Restaurant — Ordering Food

Waiter: Good evening! Are you ready to order?

Customer: Yes! I'd like a sandwich, please.

Waiter: Certainly. Would you like some fries with that?

Customer: Yes, please. And a glass of water, too.

Waiter: Sure. How many sandwiches would you like? Just one?

Customer: Yes, just one. How much is the sandwich?

Waiter: It's $8.50. Would you like some dessert? We have a piece of chocolate cake.

Customer: That sounds good! But not too much sugar, please.

Waiter: No problem! Is there anything else?

Customer: No, that's all. Thank you!

🔁 Repita com atenção: Observe "a sandwich" (countable), "some fries", "a glass of water" (measure word), "How much is..."

📘 Gramática em Ação: Countable & Uncountable
1. Countable Nouns (Substantivos Contáveis)

Têm forma singular e plural. Podem ser contados com números.

an apple → two apples | a sandwich → three sandwiches | an egg → four eggs

A. Usamos: a/an (singular), many (muitos), How many? (quantos?)

How many apples do you want? | I have many eggs. | There is a banana.

2. Uncountable Nouns (Substantivos Incontáveis)

NÃO têm forma plural. NÃO podemos contar com números.

❌ two rices | ❌ a water | ❗ rices (INCORRETO) | ✅ rice (sempre singular)

B. Usamos: much (muito), How much? (quanto?), NÃO usamos a/an

How much water do you want? | There is too much sugar. | I don't have much money.

3. Measure Words (Palavras de Medida)

Para uncountables, usamos measure words:

4. Quantifiers

a lot of / lots of — muito(a) (countable e uncountable): I eat a lot of rice. / I have lots of books.

plenty of — bastante: There is plenty of food.

enough — suficiente: Do we have enough water?

too much — demais (uncountable): There is too much salt.

too many — demais (countable): There are too many eggs.

Dica: too much + uncountable | too many + countable. Nunca diga "too much eggs"!

🎭 Prática Oral / Role-Play

Simule um pedido em restaurante! Um aluno é o garçom, o outro é o cliente.

Waiter: Good evening! Would you like to order?

Customer: Yes! I'd like a sandwich and a bowl of soup, please.

Waiter: Certainly! Would you like some bread with that?

Customer: Yes, a slice of bread, please. And a glass of water.

Waiter: How many glasses of water?

Customer: Just one, thank you. How much is the sandwich?

Waiter: It's $7.50. Anything else?

Customer: No, that's all. Can I have the check, please?

🌍 Curiosidade Cultural: Portion Sizes

Nos EUA, as porções de comida nos restaurantes são enormes! Um "small" americano pode ser maior que um "large" no Brasil. É comum levar as sobras para casa em uma caixa chamada "doggy bag".

No Reino Unido, as porções são menores. E no Brasil, é comum dividir pratos — especialmente a porção em bares!

💡 Dica: "Would you like some more?" = "Gostaria de mais?" É educado aceitar ou recusar!
