LIÇÃO 13 — Asking Questions Naturally

Nível A1
🎯 Objetivo da Lição

• Fazer perguntas no Simple Present usando Do/Does + sujeito + verbo.

• Usar don't/doesn't para formar frases negativas.

• Conhecer Question Tags básicos e dar short answers (Yes, I do / No, I don't).

🔥 Warm-Up: O que você quer saber?

Imagine que você acabou de conhecer uma pessoa. O que perguntaria?

1. Do you like sports?

2. Does she play the guitar?

3. Do they speak English?

4. What hobbies do you have?

5. Do you cook on weekends?

💡 Dica: Para perguntas no presente simples, usamos "Do" ou "Does" antes do sujeito!

🗣️ Diálogo: Interview With a New Classmate

Tom: Hi! I'm Tom. Are you new here?

Lily: Yes, I am! My name is Lily. Nice to meet you!

Tom: Nice to meet you too! Do you like sports?

Lily: Yes, I do! I love volleyball. Do you play volleyball?

Tom: No, I don't. But I play soccer. Does your brother play sports?

Lily: Yes, he does. He plays basketball. Do you like reading?

Tom: I don't read much, but I like drawing. You're Brazilian, aren't you?

Lily: Yes, I am! He plays soccer, doesn't he?

Tom: Yes, he does! Do you cook Brazilian food?

Lily: Sometimes! I usually cook feijoada on weekends.

🔁 Repita com atenção: Leia o diálogo em voz alta. Preste atenção nas perguntas com Do/Does e nos question tags!

📘 Gramática em Ação: Questions & Negatives
1. Perguntas com Do / Does

Estrutura: Do/Does + sujeito + verbo (base form) + ?

Do you like sports? | Does she play the guitar? | Do they study English? | Does he cook?

2. Frases Negativas com Don't / Doesn't

Estrutura: Sujeito + don't/doesn't + verbo (base form)

I don't like coffee. | She doesn't play soccer. | We don't watch TV. | He doesn't speak French.

A. Atenção: Com doesn't, o verbo volta à forma base (sem -s!):

❌ She doesn't plays → ✅ She doesn't play | ❌ He doesn't goes → ✅ He doesn't go

3. Short Answers (Respostas Curtas)
PerguntaAfirmativaNegativa
Do you like sports?Yes, I do.No, I don't.
Does she play guitar?Yes, she does.No, she doesn't.
Do they study English?Yes, they do.No, they don't.
Does he cook?Yes, he does.No, he doesn't.
4. Question Tags (Introdução)

São perguntinhas no final da frase para confirmar algo.

A. Com verbo to be:

You're Brazilian, aren't you? | She is your friend, isn't she?

B. Com Simple Present: usa don't/doesn't no tag

He plays soccer, doesn't he? | You like pizza, don't you?

Dica: O question tag é o oposto da frase principal. Frase positiva → tag negativo. Frase negativa → tag positivo.

🎭 Prática Oral / Role-Play

A: Hi! Do you like sports?

B: Yes, I do! I love volleyball. Do you play any sport?

A: I don't play sports, but I like drawing. Does your sister draw?

B: No, she doesn't. She prefers singing.

A: You like music, don't you?

B: Yes, I do! I play the guitar.

A: That's cool! Do you practice every day?

B: I usually practice on weekends.

👅 Trava-língua

Do you dare to dance daily at dawn?

🌍 Curiosidade Cultural: Popular Sports

No Reino Unido, o cricket e o rugby são muito populares, além do futebol (football). Nos EUA, o American football é o mais assistido — o Super Bowl atrai mais de 100 milhões de espectadores! O basketball e o baseball também são populares. Na Austrália, o Australian rules football é o esporte nacional.

💡 Dica: Americanos dizem "soccer" para futebol e "football" para futebol americano. Britânicos dizem "football" para o nosso futebol!
