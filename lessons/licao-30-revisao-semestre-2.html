LIÇÃO 30 — Revisão Geral do Semestre 2 🎓

Nível A1/A2
🎯 Objetivo da Lição

• Revisar todos os conteúdos do Semestre 2: Simple Past, Going To, Will, Comparatives, Superlatives, Present Perfect, Modal Verbs, Phrasal Verbs.

• Ler uma história integrada que utiliza praticamente todos os conteúdos do semestre.

• Completar exercícios abrangentes e um projeto final.

📖 História Integrada: A Year of Changes

Leia a história abaixo com atenção. Ela contém exemplos de todos os tópicos estudados neste semestre!

Part 1: The Past

Last year, my life changed completely. First, I lived (Simple Past — regular) in a small town called Campinas. I worked at a local store and studied English at night. Then, I went (irregular) to São Paulo for a job interview. I was very nervous, but I did my best. I met new people and saw amazing places. I ate at wonderful restaurants and bought souvenirs for my family. I didn't know (negativa) that São Paulo was so big! I thought it was scary, but it wasn't. After that visit, I made a big decision.

Part 2: The Move

I was going to move to São Paulo (Going To — plano). I was going to start a new life there. My parents said I should (Modal — conselho) think carefully. "You must (Modal — obrigação) save money first," my dad said. "You have to (Modal — obrigação externa) find an apartment before you go," my mom added. I shouldn't rush, but I was excited!

Part 3: The Future

"I'll call you every day!" I promised (Will — promessa). "I won't forget you!" My sister said, "I'll visit you on weekends!" (Will — promessa). My best friend said, "I might come too!" (Might — possibilidade). The day I left, I said, "I'll be fine!" (Will — certeza). When I arrived in São Paulo, it was raining. "It'll be sunny tomorrow," I thought (Will — previsão).

Part 4: Comparing

São Paulo is bigger than Campinas (Comparative). It's more expensive too! But Campinas is more peaceful. The traffic in São Paulo is worse than in Campinas. However, São Paulo has the best restaurants (Superlative) and the most interesting cultural events. My apartment is smaller than my old house, but it's as comfortable as home (as...as). It's not as quiet as Campinas, though!

Part 5: Experiences

I have lived in São Paulo for six months now (Present Perfect). I have met wonderful people. I have already visited three museums. I have just started a new course. I have never eaten such good pizza! But I haven't found a good bakery yet. Have you ever been to São Paulo? It's an amazing city!

Part 6: Daily Life

Every morning, I wake up early and get up at 6 a.m. I put on my clothes and turn on the radio. I look for my keys (they're always lost!). I pick up my bag and go out. After work, I come in, take off my shoes, sit down and relax. I turn off the TV and hang up the phone. Sometimes I find out interesting things about the city. I always give back books to the library on time!

Part 7: Reflections

Looking back, I was afraid of change. I thought I couldn't do it (Could — passado). But I should have trusted myself! Life in the big city has been an adventure. First, I was scared. Then, I adapted. After that, I started to love it. Finally, I can say: this was the best decision I have ever made! In the end, change is good. I 'm going to keep learning and growing. I 'll never stop!

📝 Exercícios de Revisão
A. Múltipla Escolha — 25 questões
1. She ___ to school yesterday. (ir)
2. I ___ going to study tonight.
3. The phone is ringing! I ___ answer it!
4. São Paulo is ___ than Campinas.
5. I have ___ visited Paris. (nunca)
6. You ___ wear a seatbelt. (obrigação)
7. ___ the TV, please. (desligar)
8. ___ you ever ___ sushi?
9. She ___ her coat and went out.
10. This is the ___ movie I've ever seen!
11. I didn't ___ to the party. (ir)
12. ___ she going to travel?
13. I promise I ___ be late!
14. English is ___ than Chinese. (fácil)
15. She has ___ arrived. (acabou de)
16. You ___ eat more vegetables. (conselho)
17. I'm ___ my keys. (procurar)
18. He ___ the book yesterday. (ler)
19. It ___ rain later. (possibilidade)
20. She is ___ tall ___ her brother.
21. I haven't finished ___. (ainda)
22. ___ you help me, please?
23. They ___ to the beach last weekend.
24. ___ I sit here? (permissão formal)
25. ___ your shoes before entering.

D. Listening Activities — 5 atividades (autoestudo)

Instruções para prática de escuta. Use vídeos, podcasts ou músicas em inglês!

1. Ouça uma música em inglês e anote todos os phrasal verbs que conseguir identificar.

2. Assista a um vídeo curto (2-3 min) e anote todos os verbos no passado que ouvir.

3. Ouça um podcast e identifique ao menos 3 comparativos ou superlativos.

4. Assista a uma cena de filme e anote todos os modais (should, must, could, may, might).

5. Ouça uma entrevista e anote exemplos de Present Perfect (have/has + particípio).

E. Speaking Activities — 5 atividades

Pratique falando! Grave-se se possível.

1. Conte a história das suas últimas férias em 2 minutos usando Simple Past e sequenciadores.

2. Descreva seus planos para o próximo ano usando going to e will.

3. Compare duas cidades que você conhece usando comparativos e superlativos.

4. Dê 5 conselhos para alguém aprendendo inglês usando should/shouldn't.

5. Descreva sua rotina matinal usando pelo menos 5 phrasal verbs.

F. Redação — 200-250 palavras

Escreva um texto sobre "My English Learning Journey" usando conteúdos do semestre inteiro.

Use: Simple Past (o que você fez), Going To (planos), Will (promessas), Comparatives (antes e depois), Present Perfect (experiências), Modal Verbs (conselhos), Phrasal Verbs (rotina de estudos).

G. Projeto Final: My English Learning Presentation

Crie uma apresentação sobre sua jornada de aprendizado de inglês!

Inclua: 1) Uma timeline do que você aprendeu; 2) Seus objetivos futuros (going to / will); 3) Conselhos para outros estudantes (should); 4) Uma rotina de estudos usando phrasal verbs; 5) Comparação entre seu inglês antes e agora (comparatives/superlatives); 6) Experiências que você teve com o inglês (Present Perfect).
