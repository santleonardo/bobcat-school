🔥 Warm-Up: Você saberia se virar?

Imagine que você está em um país de língua inglesa. O que você diria nestas situações?

1. Você está perdido na rua → Excuse me, how do I get to the station?

2. Você não entendeu algo → Could you repeat that, please?

3. Você quer fazer um pedido no restaurante → I'd like a table for two, please.

4. Você precisa de remédio → I have a headache. Do you have any medicine?

5. Você quer saber o preço → How much is this?

💡 Dica: "Excuse me" e "Please" são mágicos em inglês! Sempre os use para ser educado.

🗣️ Diálogo: A Tourist's First Day in New York

Tourist: Excuse me! I'm looking for the subway station. How do I get there?

Local: Go straight for two blocks, then turn left. It's on the right.

Tourist: Thank you! Could you speak more slowly, please?

Local: Sure! Go straight... two blocks... turn left. It's on the right.

Tourist: Got it! How far is it?

Local: About 5 minutes. Are you a tourist?

Tourist: Yes, I am! It's my first day in New York. I'm staying at the Grand Hotel.

Local: Welcome! You're going to love it here!

Tourist: Thank you so much! I'm lost, but now I know the way!

Local: No problem! Enjoy your trip!

🔁 Repita com atenção: Este diálogo usa várias expressões de sobrevivência que vamos praticar!
✈️ Situações Reais de Comunicação

Clique em cada situação para ver os diálogos e expressões essenciais! 👇

You: Excuse me, where is gate 12?

Agent: Gate 12 is on the second floor. Go straight and turn left.

You: Thank you! And where can I check in?

Agent: The check-in counter is right over there.

You: Can I see your passport, please?

Agent: Here you are.

Key: Excuse me, where is...? / Can I check in? / Here you are.

You: Hello. I have a reservation. My name is Silva.

Receptionist: Welcome! Can I see your ID, please?

You: Sure. Can I have the Wi-Fi password?

Receptionist: Of course. It's on your key card. What time is breakfast?

You: Breakfast is from 7 to 10 a.m. Enjoy your stay!

Key: I have a reservation. / Can I have...? / What time is breakfast?

You: I'd like a table for two, please.

Waiter: Of course. Here is the menu.

You: Can I have the chicken and a glass of water, please?

Waiter: Certainly! Would you like some dessert?

You: No, thank you. Could I have the check, please?

Key: I'd like... / Can I have...? / Could I have the check, please?

You: Excuse me, I have a headache. Do you have any medicine?

Pharmacist: Yes, we have these pills. Take two with water.

You: How much is it?

Pharmacist: It's $5.99.

You: Do I need a prescription?

Pharmacist: No, you don't. This is over-the-counter.

Key: I have a headache / Do you have any...? / How much is it? / Do I need a prescription?

You: Excuse me, where can I find the milk?

Employee: It's in aisle 3, on the left.

You: Thank you! Can I pay with a credit card?

Employee: Yes, of course.

You: How much are the apples?

Employee: $2.50 a pound.

Key: Where can I find...? / Can I pay with...? / How much is/are...?

You: Excuse me, how do I get to the train station?

Person: Go straight for two blocks, then turn right.

You: Could you speak more slowly, please?

Person: Sure. Go straight... then turn right. It's on the left.

You: Is it far from here?

Person: No, it's about 5 minutes.

Key: How do I get to...? / Could you speak more slowly? / Is it far? / I'm lost.

🌍 Curiosidade Cultural: Tipping Culture

Nos EUA, a gorjeta (tip) é quase obrigatória! Em restaurantes, espera-se 15-20% do valor da conta. Garçons dependem de gorjetas para sobreviver — o salário base é muito baixo!

Também se deixa gorjeta para: taxistas (10-15%), cabeleireiros (15-20%), e funcionários de hotel ($1-5 por serviço). No Reino Unido, a gorjeta é menos obrigatória mas apreciada.

No Brasil, a gorjeta (10%) geralmente já está incluída na conta. Mas nos EUA, você precisa calcular e adicionar manualmente!

💡 Dica: Para calcular 20% de gorjeta rapidamente, mova a vírgula uma casa e multiplique por 2! Ex: conta $45 → $4.50 × 2 = $9.00 de gorjeta.
